Research Outline
Working Title
Exposing children to programming is beneficial.
Introduction
Introducing programming to children at an early age provides them with certain advantages. Specifically, it improves their cognitive ability or problem-solving skills, trains them on various computer concepts to prepare them for future computer-intensive jobs, and teaches them valuable interpersonal or social skills.
Body
Argument 1
Programming lessons reinforce and refine a child's ability to solve problems; thus making them better at facing the obstacles they face on a day-to-day basis.

Argument 2
Programming increases children’s computer proficiency, which prepares them to join the modern workplaces.

Argument 3
Children who are actively exposed to programming learn to cultivate important human values, namely leadership, creativity, diligence and curiosity. Human values such as these are important for building character and personal development.

Counterargument & Rebuttal
Learning programming at a young age can be done by using a variety of methods to get youngsters to gain a firmer grasp on programming and in turn gain the skills to further themselves in their future. However, starting programming too early is counterproductive as children have not yet reached the cognitive ability to comprehend it well enough.

Conclusion
In conclusion, introducing programming to children at an early age provides them with significant advantages, including improved cognitive abilities and problem-solving skills, preparation for future computer-intensive jobs through training in various computer concepts, and the development of valuable interpersonal and social skills. Programming is therefore beneficial to young children and should be introduced to them in their early years in a playful manner to incorporate skills that give them an advantage in additional skill sets. This will allow them to enjoy the benefits of learning programming at a young age, in the later parts of life.
